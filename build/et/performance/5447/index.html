Idea, choreography, performance: Annika Viibus, KardoOjassalu, Evelyn Piirak, Karoline Suhhov
Visual artist, lighting design: Rommi Ruttas
Sound design and live presentation: Argo Vals
Costume design: Karin Sülluste
Photo: Laura Rohtlaan
Length: ~60'
Premiere: 20.02.2018, Teater Endla

Ticket: on presale at Piletilevi 8/12 EUR, at the door 10/15 EUR

Vocal part is in Estonian.

“Those whispers, which come to you, have been chosen for you. You are placed in a certain group. Your orientations and interests are made clear. You are only fed (we know it, because we live in those whispers) the information, that seems to be the single possible information and a whisper is created to cover up the ohterone. A person/human always wants to identify, unite or kontrast to something and then he/she acts accordingly. Education, culture and the mind will helpy ou. There is no ohter way.”
(Inspiration for the performance originates from radio KUKU’s broadcast Välismääraja 7.10.2018)



Annika Viibus graduated University of Tartu Viljandi Culture Academy in 2009 as a dance teacher and choreographer. After graduating the University she has produced atleast 1-2 performances per year (half of them as co-productions). The most bright ones from past years are „"Aatomiku juhtumused" (2012), "Idüll" (2013), "OmaOma" (2013) and "Vabadus vabanduseks" (2015). She lives in Pärnu,, though she works in Pärnumaa and Läänemaa.

Karoline Suhhov graduated University of Tartu Viljandi Culture Academy in 2015 as a dance teacher and choreographer. Her works are influenced a fleeting time in Concordia University. She has been seen in many dance performances that have been produced in Pärnu and the latest work as a choreographer was „Suitsune Transs Tinapaberis“ (2016). She's is from Pärnu, but works at different places all over Estonia.

Kardo Ojassalu graduated University of Tartu Viljandi Culture Academy in 2010 as a dance teacher and choreographer. In 2017 he acquired blacksmithing speciality. He has been a choreographer to performances like Jane Eyre“ (Rakvere teater,2012), „Kangelapsed“ (Endla teater, 2015), „Et sa kaotaksid kõik“ (Endla teater,2016), „Säärane mulk“ (Endla teater, 2017). He lives and (mainly) works in Pärnu.

Evelyn Piirak graduated University of Tartu Viljandi Culture Academy as a dance teacher and choreographer and acquired cosmetics speciality. After graduating the University she has produced two dance performances: "Siselend Wiesbadeni"(2016) and "Viipekeel kui tants"(2015). She has also created choreography for performances at Ugala Theatre, e.g. „Moraal“(2016). In 2017 a dance she created („Päikesetervitus“) was used in XII Youth Dance Celebration. She lives and works in Pärnu, Pärnumaa.

The most importants push in Argo Vals' education was in 2006, when he started to study in University of Tartu Viljandi Culture Academy. There he was introduced tototally new environment with unexpected twists and turns. There he found connections to dance performances and got interested in connecting his soundings to other art forms. He saw that dancers have somekind of interesting and different way of being, therefore he is in atleast two dance performance processes every year. He lives in Tallinn, and works at different places all over Estonia.

Probably the most important push in Rommi Ruttas' educational road was in 2011, when he did not get accepted to University of Tartu Viljandi Culture Academy theatre arts as... an actor. He said that while studying lighting design, all your life is doing dance performances. He lives in Viljandi, works at different places all over Estonia.

Karin Sülluste graduated Tallinn's Old Town Sewing School in 1994, with a diploma to women's outerwear tailor. Since spring 2016 she has her own clothing line ILM by Karin.
